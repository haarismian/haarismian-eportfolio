Collaborative Discussion 2

The paper provides some information on the strengths of taking a metamodel approach to support object-oriented design of the Internet of Things (IoT) systems. Specifically, the use of metamodels can help to model IoT system properties at different levels of abstraction. At the analysis phase, metamodels can be used to abstract the main entities of the IoT system (such as human/digital users and smart objects), their basic features, and high-level interactions. This can help to provide a common understanding among stakeholders about the system being developed. At the design phase, metamodels can be used to define more detailed aspects of the system, such as its architecture and communication protocols. This can help to ensure that all components of the system are designed in a consistent manner. At the implementation phase, metamodels can be used to generate code automatically or semi-automatically based on the models created in earlier phases. This can help to reduce errors and improve efficiency in coding.

One potential weakness of taking a metamodel approach for IoT systems, as mentioned in the PDF file, is the risk of over-engineering and creating overly complex models that are difficult to understand and maintain. The article suggests that this risk can be mitigated by using a modular approach to metamodel development. Specifically, the proposed approach defines three levels of SO metamodels, respectively linked to the analysis, design, and implementation phases of development. Each level is designed to be modular and reusable across different projects. By using a modular approach to metamodel development, the article suggests that it is possible to avoid over-engineering and create models that are easier to understand and maintain. This is because each module can be developed independently and tested separately before being integrated into the larger system. However, it is important to note that even with a modular approach, there is still a risk of creating overly complex models if they are not designed carefully. Therefore, it is important for developers to strike a balance between creating models that are detailed enough to support the development process but not so complex that they become difficult to manage.

References:

Fortino, G., Guerrieri, A., Russo, W. & Savaglio, C. (2015). Towards a Development Methodology for Smart Object-Oriented IoT Systems: A Metamodel Approach. 2015 IEEE International Conference on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics. 1297-1302. DOI: 10.1109/SMC.2015.231. [Accessed 15 April 2023]
