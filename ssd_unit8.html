Learning Objectives

  • Understand and explain the principles, technology, and use of cryptography, particularly in the context of operating systems.
  • Explore and describe common cryptographic libraries and the issues encountered with them.
  • Analyze the pros and cons of using common cryptographic libraries.

  • Demonstrate the use of cryptographic libraries in a simple application by integrating them effectively and explaining the process.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

All in all, the learning outcomes of this module will equip me with vital skills that will significantly enhance my capabilities as a software engineer.

Why did you select the algorithm you chose?

I chose the Fernet symmetric encryption method because it is a strong, high-level method provided by the cryptography library in Python. Fernet guarantees that data encrypted using it cannot be further manipulated or read without the key. It uses AES for encryption and HMAC for authentication, providing a secure way to handle encryption. I have also used this system of cryptography in the group project as well.

Would it meet the GDPR regulations? Justify your answer.

Yes, using encryption to secure personal data is one of the measures suggested by GDPR to ensure the safety and integrity of personal data. Fernet, being a reliable and strong encryption algorithm, would help in fulfilling this requirement. However, it's important to remember that GDPR compliance is not only about how data is encrypted, but also about how keys are managed, how consent is obtained for data processing, how data subjects can exercise their rights, and much more.
