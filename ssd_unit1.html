Unit 1

Learning Objectives

  • Unit 1 introduces secure software development, highlighting waterfall and agile methods, risk-aware culture, and the Unified Modelling Language (UML) in software design.
  • The unit provides learning outcomes such as identifying literature on secure software development, creating UML flow charts, and recognizing the importance of risk awareness in organizations.
  • Students are expected to expand upon basic coding techniques for their final team project, with progress demonstrated in seminar sessions.
  • Activities for students include creating a GitHub e-portfolio, participating in seminars, and engaging in collaborative discussions about UML flowcharts.
  • The introductory seminar, "Introduction to Team Project", includes team meetings and is a significant part of the unit.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 1 of the Secure Software Development module has presented me with insightful perspectives about various software development methodologies. Diving deep into the agile and waterfall models has enabled me to comprehend their applicability and implications when developing secure software. Additionally, learning about the Unified Modelling Language (UML) and its use in software development has helped me build a solid foundation for understanding software deployment models from their design phase rather than just coding. The practical aspect of UML, explored through the creation of a flowchart, has directly addressed the learning objective of decomposing an activity into tasks that can be represented using UML techniques.

Moreover, the unit has emphasized the importance of cultivating a risk-aware culture within an organisation, which is an aspect I hadn't fully considered before. This concept has challenged me to think beyond the technical aspects of creating secure software, to consider the human elements involved in addressing security challenges. By actively participating discussions about UML and risk-aware culture, I am gaining a better understanding of the holistic approach necessary to develop secure software, thereby aligning my practical activities with the unit's learning objectives. These experiences are preparing me not only to tackle the module's coding activities but also to contribute effectively to my team's final submission.
