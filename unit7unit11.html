Autonomous car design and implementation assignments

As a postgraduate level computer science student, I recently completed two challenging assignments that required me to design and implement a driverless car using Python. The experience was both challenging and enlightening, and I am excited to reflect on the valuable lessons that I learned through these assignments.

First and foremost, I learned the importance of good planning. As the Abraham Lincoln quote goes, "Give me six hours to chop down a tree, and I will spend the first four sharpening the axe." This quote perfectly captures the significance of spending ample time planning and utilizing various modeling techniques to create a comprehensive vision of the system in one's mind. By doing so, I was able to facilitate the subsequent implementation phase significantly.

During the implementation phase, I discovered several edge cases that I hadn't considered during the planning phase. However, having a solid plan and an excellent understanding of different modeling techniques enabled me to address these challenges effectively. I came to appreciate the value of various modeling techniques and how they significantly facilitate the coding process, similar to how writing an outline for an essay helps in writing the essay itself.

Moreover, I learned through the planning phase that it is easier to visualize systems than to write them out. By visualizing the system using different modeling techniques, I was able to gain a much better understanding of the system's components, which made coding much easier.

The assignments also highlighted the challenges of scaling implementations. For instance, the sensor class was easy to design, but scaling it to accommodate different types of sensors during implementation was much harder. Additionally, I learned the value of test cases, which are essential in ensuring that implementations are rigorously tested and free of errors. Going forward, I will aim to incorporate test cases into my implementation plans.

Through these assignments, I also got the chance to develop my skills in OOP, learn different data structures, and algorithms. This experience will undoubtedly prove valuable in my future endeavors as a software engineer.

Regarding how I would implement the lessons I learned in my professional life, I would prioritize planning and visualization, utilizing various modeling techniques to create a comprehensive understanding of the system. Additionally, I would incorporate test cases into my implementation plans to ensure that implementations are thoroughly tested and free of errors.

If I had more time to work on these assignments, I would focus on implementing advanced decision-making algorithms that factor in road conditions, weather, and traffic patterns. Additionally, I would explore integrating interfaces for human interaction and control, allowing passengers to give commands and override the car's decisions in certain situations.

In conclusion, the experience of designing and implementing a driverless car using Python was both challenging and enlightening. Through the assignments, I learned the importance of good planning, the value of various modeling techniques, the challenges of scaling implementations, and the significance of test cases. I am excited to apply these lessons in my future endeavors and continue developing my skills in OOP, data structures, and algorithms.
