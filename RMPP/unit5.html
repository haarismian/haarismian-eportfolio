Unit 2

Learning Objectives

  • Introduction to Interview and Survey Methods: Learn the fundamentals of conducting in-depth interviews and designing surveys as primary data collection techniques.
  • Understanding Population and Sampling: Explore the concepts of population and sample in the context of research, vital for designing and conducting effective surveys and interviews.
  • Application of Pre- and Post-Testing: Get introduced to pre- and post-testing methods as tools to assess the impact of interventions or new processes in a research setting.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Week 5 was instrumental in broadening my understanding of how interviews and surveys can be effectively integrated into research. The detailed exploration of these methods showed me how each can be tailored to suit different research questions and objectives (Creswell and Creswell, 2018). Learning about the importance of defining a clear population and sample was particularly informative, as it underscored the necessity of precision in research design to yield actionable insights (Andrade, 2020). Additionally, the concept of pre- and post-testing provided a clear framework for observing outcomes before and after implementing a new process or system (Sitzia and Wood, 1998). I now feel more confident in my ability to utilize these methods to gather comprehensive data and generate meaningful results in my future research endeavors, particularly in the field of computer science where user feedback and system evaluation are crucial (Robson, 2011).
