Learning Objectives

  • Understanding Quantitative Data Analysis: Introduce the different levels of measurement for quantitative data, and the importance of understanding these to accurately analyze data.
  • Introduction to Statistical Measures: Learn about measures of location and spread which are fundamental to describing and summarizing data.
  • Foundations of Hypothesis Testing: Explore the concept of inference and the steps involved in hypothesis testing to make informed conclusions about data.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 8 has significantly deepened my understanding of inferential statistics, which is pivotal for deriving meaningful conclusions from data in research. The introduction to different levels of data measurement and the detailed discussion on measures of location and spread provided the foundational knowledge necessary to properly summarize and interpret data. Furthermore, the focus on hypothesis testing illuminated how to formally test assumptions and theories about data populations. This week's insights into statistical inference and the uncertainty inherent in data have equipped me with the tools to more confidently approach and analyze research data, ensuring that my conclusions are not only insightful but also statistically valid.
