Learning Objectives

  • Understanding Questionnaire Design: Gain insights into the nuances of designing a questionnaire, distinguishing it from surveys, and learning about the methods of obtaining responses.
  • Evaluation of Questionnaire Quality: Learn to differentiate between effective and poorly constructed questionnaires and understand the implications for data quality and research outcomes.
  • Data Analysis Techniques: Acquire skills on how to analyze responses from different types of questions within a questionnaire, whether they are open-ended, closed, or a mix, depending on the research method applied.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 6 was a deep dive into the craft of questionnaire design, a fundamental tool in data collection for research. It clarified the critical distinctions between a questionnaire and a survey, emphasizing the role of the questionnaire as an instrument within broader survey methodologies. This week, I learned the importance of question type selection—open versus closed—and the impact this has on the type of data collected, whether quantitative, qualitative, or mixed. Understanding the common pitfalls in questionnaire design and how to avoid them has prepared me to create more effective research instruments. This knowledge is crucial for my upcoming projects where precise data collection is vital for sound research conclusions.
