Unit 6

Learning Objectives

  • Understanding Questionnaire Design: Gain insights into the nuances of designing a questionnaire, distinguishing it from surveys, and learning about the methods of obtaining responses.
  • Evaluation of Questionnaire Quality: Learn to differentiate between effective and poorly constructed questionnaires and understand the implications for data quality and research outcomes.
  • Data Analysis Techniques: Acquire skills on how to analyze responses from different types of questions within a questionnaire, whether they are open-ended, closed, or a mix, depending on the research method applied.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 6 provided a comprehensive exploration of questionnaire design, a crucial tool in research data collection. It elucidated the key differences between questionnaires and surveys, highlighting the questionnaire's role as an instrument within broader survey methodologies (Ng, 2006). The unit emphasized the importance of question type selection—open versus closed—and its impact on data collection, whether quantitative, qualitative, or mixed (Fowler, 2009). Understanding common pitfalls in questionnaire design, such as leading questions and inadequate response options, and learning how to avoid them has equipped me to create more effective research instruments (Satrix Solutions, 2024). This knowledge is essential for upcoming projects where precise data collection is crucial for drawing sound research conclusions, particularly in the field of computer science where data quality directly impacts analysis outcomes (OECD, 2012).
