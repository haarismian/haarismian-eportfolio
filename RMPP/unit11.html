Learning Objectives

  • Understand the purpose of research, including the main reasons such as exploring, describing, and explaining topics and situations.
  • Gain familiarity with the scientific method, including the processes of observation, hypothesis formation, reasoning, and testing, and how these elements differ across disciplines.
  • Differentiate between deductive and inductive reasoning, and comprehend their applications and limitations in research settings.
  • Develop an understanding of research ethics, focusing on principles like respect, beneficence, justice, and adherence to law and public interest, and their importance in conducting responsible research.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Reflecting on the first week of our module, I feel I've started to really grasp the essentials of research and the scientific method. Understanding why research is carried out—to explore, describe, and explain—has genuinely sparked my curiosity about how these concepts apply both in academia and in the real world. The lessons on deductive and inductive reasoning were particularly eye-opening. Seeing the practical differences between these reasoning styles and how they're applied, especially through examples like the diagram from Miessler (2020), made the concepts click for me. Choosing a topic for our literature review as part of the assessments encouraged me to think deeper and more critically about my own research direction. This process, coupled with the strong emphasis on ethics—like ensuring the dignity and rights of research participants—has made me more aware of the responsibility we hold as researchers. I now appreciate the importance of ethical conduct and transparency more than ever, which will definitely shape how I approach my future projects.
