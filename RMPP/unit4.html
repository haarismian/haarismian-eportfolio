Unit 4

Learning Objectives

  • Introduction to Key Qualitative and Quantitative Data Collection Methods: Learn about case studies, focus groups, and both qualitative and quantitative observation techniques.
  • Analysis of Methodological Strengths and Limitations: Understand the advantages and drawbacks associated with each data collection method discussed in this unit.
  • Applicability to Research: Determine the suitability of these methods for specific research investigations and anticipate the types of data each method will yield.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 4 was a deep dive into understanding how different data collection methods such as case studies, focus groups, and observations can enhance the richness of research findings. This week's content clarified the nuances between qualitative and quantitative observations and their implications on research outcomes. By engaging with case studies and focus groups, I appreciated the depth they bring to research, especially in understanding complex phenomena. However, the discussions also highlighted the limitations of each method, such as potential biases in case studies and the non-generalizability of findings from focus groups. I now feel more equipped to choose the right method for my research objectives, understanding not just the benefits but also the critical considerations needed to maintain research integrity (Yin, 2009; Freitas et al., 2017;)
