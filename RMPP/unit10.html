Learning Objectives

    • Develop a thorough understanding of research writing, focusing on its structure, significance in technical communication, and application in professional academic documents like dissertations and research proposals.
    • Prepare for dissertation writing by exploring the different sections of a dissertation and learning how to approach each effectively.
    • Apply theoretical knowledge through practical activities, such as the preparation of a research proposal and the integration of statistical analysis into an e-Portfolio, to enhance research proficiency.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 10 has significantly improved my understanding of research writing, particularly how pivotal it is for articulating complex technical data effectively. Learning the structure of a dissertation and the approach to each section has equipped me with a clearer roadmap for my own dissertation journey which is coming soon. The practical exercises, especially constructing a concise research proposal, have been invaluable. They've not only honed my ability to frame and tackle research problems but have also deepened my appreciation for meticulous planning in research endeavors. This unit bridges the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application, a crucial leap for my academic and professional development in the tech industry.
