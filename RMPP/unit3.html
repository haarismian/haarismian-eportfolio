Learning Objectives

  • Introduction to Research Design Types: Understand the two main types of research designs: exploratory and descriptive, and how they influence the research process.
  • Familiarization with Research Methods: Gain knowledge about the three primary research methods (quantitative, qualitative, and mixed methods), along with associated data collection techniques such as experiments, surveys, and case studies.
  • Application of Research Methods to Specific Areas: Develop the ability to select appropriate research and data collection methods suited to my specific area of research.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Unit 3 of my MSC course on Research Methods, I really got a handle on the practical steps for conducting research. The breakdown of exploratory and conclusive research designs was especially useful; it clarified how each approach serves a different purpose depending on what you're trying to find out. By learning about qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods, I now feel better prepared to pick the right method for my research questions. This unit has directly impacted how I plan my research projects, making me more thoughtful about matching my research methods with my goals. It's reassuring to have these strategies in my toolkit for future work.

Response to research questions:

Please note I am completing this artefact after I finished the research proposal so my response is written retroactively

1. Suitable Research Methods:

Given the aims and objectives of my proposal, a Mixed Methods Research approach seems most suitable. This approach integrates both qualitative and quantitative methods, ideal for my project as it involves evaluating the quantitative aspects of detection accuracy and the qualitative aspects of user interactions and responses to interventions.

I'll use Quantitative methods to statistically assess the accuracy of the GPT-4 model in identifying instances of cyberbullying, measure response effectiveness, and compare the performance of GPT-4 against existing solutions. This will include experiments designed to quantify the true positive and false positive rates of detection.

Qualitative methods will provide deeper insights into how users perceive the interventions of the GPT-4 enabled chatbots and how these interventions impact their experience on social media platforms. I plan to use interviews or focus groups to gather detailed responses about user satisfaction and the practical effects of the chatbot interventions.

2. Data Collection Methods:

For my research, I will use the following data collection methods:

  • Surveys and Polls: To gather broad quantitative data on user experiences and satisfaction levels with the GPT-4 chatbot.
  • Interviews: To collect in-depth qualitative insights from a selected group of users who have interacted with the chatbot. This will help me understand personal user experiences and the subjective effectiveness of the chatbot's interventions.
  • Experiments: To conduct controlled tests assessing the detection capabilities and intervention success rates of the chatbot. This will involve scenarios where the chatbot is exposed to simulated social media environments to evaluate its performance in real-time conditions.
  • Observations: To monitor real-time interactions on social media platforms where the GPT-4 chatbot is active, allowing me to observe its functionality and user engagement directly.

These methods will provide crucial data that aligns with the different aspects of my research objectives, offering a comprehensive view of both the technical efficacy and user reception of the GPT-4 based cyberbullying mitigation strategies.
