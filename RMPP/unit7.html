Unit 7

Learning Objectives

  • Foundational Concepts of Research Quality: Understand the key concepts of validity, generalizability, and reliability and their crucial roles in research design and execution.
  • Analysis and Presentation of Data: Learn the differences between qualitative and quantitative data, including methods for analyzing and presenting results effectively.
  • Preparation for Data Collection: Recognize the preparatory steps necessary before data collection such as data cleansing and validation to ensure the integrity of the research process.

Reflections of the activity and course learning objectives

Week 7 of the course provided a comprehensive overview of validity, generalizability, and reliabilityâ€”three pillars that are fundamental to any research study (Saran, 2018). Understanding these concepts has significantly influenced my approach to research design, ensuring that the data I collect and the conclusions I draw are robust and defensible. This unit also deepened my understanding of the nuances between qualitative and quantitative data, particularly in how they are analyzed and presented (National University, 2023). This knowledge is vital as I prepare to cleanse and validate my data before embarking on detailed analysis (Atlan, 2023). I now feel better equipped to structure my research to not only answer my questions but to ensure that the findings can be confidently applied to wider contexts (Grand Canyon University, 2023).
